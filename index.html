01 — welcome

Welcome!

This site began as a personal notebook, one built by a high-school student who fell in love with astrophysics and never quite recovered. It's grown into something bigger: a single, organized home for the books, problem sets, projects, and videos that make the universe a little easier to reach.

Learning astronomy on your own can feel overwhelming — a great lecture here, a good book there, a worksheet you can never find again. SpaceSparks exists to put all of that in one place, sorted and ready, so the only thing left to do is get curious. Whether you're just noticing the moon for the first time or already chasing exoplanet data, there's something here for you.

02 — about me

Who's behind this?

Who I Am

I'm a high-school student who is passionate about space, loves to read and stays up far too late to see a meteor shower. I started this project because I wanted a place where people like me (curious, a little overwhelmed, not sure where to start) could follow that curiosity without getting lost.

My Goals

  • ◈ Study astrophysics at university and go deep into the questions that keep me up at night.
  • ◈ Take part in real research, even in small ways.
  • ◈ Inspire other students to see science as something they can actually do, not just admire.
  • ◈ Make astronomy genuinely accessible — no gatekeeping.
